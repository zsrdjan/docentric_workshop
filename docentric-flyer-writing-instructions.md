# Docentric Flyer Writing Instructions for Claude

## Overview
These instructions guide the creation of Docentric Customer Workshops flyers. Two versions are needed for different audiences:
1. **Customer-facing**: For end-customers considering Docentric workshops
2. **Partner-facing**: For partners who will deliver workshops to customers

## Docentric Voice and Writing Principles

All Docentric communications must follow these three core principles:

### 1. Warm, Friendly and Helpful
- Use inclusive language ("your team", "we", "together")
- Show enthusiasm and encouragement
- Be supportive and approachable
- Examples: "Ready to lift off?", "Celebrate your achievement!", "We start by understanding your needs"

### 2. Direct, Concise and Specific
- Get to the point quickly
- Use concrete numbers and details (session counts, hours, deliverables)
- Avoid vague promises - be specific about what customers get

### 3. Visually Clear
- Use headers and subheaders generously
- Break content into scannable sections
- Use bullet points for lists
- Use visual markers (âœ“, checkmarks, dividers like ---)
- Keep paragraphs short
- Use bold for emphasis on key points

## Customer-Facing Flyer Structure

### 1. Title
**Purpose**: Capture attention and essence of the workshops

**Guidelines**:
- Should be catchy and memorable
- Clearly indicate it's a workshop/training program
- Can include a tagline or subtitle
- Examples: "Lift Off Workshop", "Jumpstart Program", "Success Workshop"

### 2. Introduction
**Purpose**: Explain why these workshops exist and their main value (from customer perspective)

**Guidelines**:
- Start with the customer's pain point or goal
- Clearly state what the workshop helps them achieve
- Mention any prerequisites upfront
- 2-3 short paragraphs maximum
- Focus on the "why" before the "what"

**Key questions to answer**:
- Why should a customer care about this workshop?
- What problem does it solve?
- Who is this for?

### 3. Body

#### Content Description
**Purpose**: Detail what the workshop includes

**Guidelines**:
- Break down the workshop into clear phases/steps
- For each component, include:
  - What it is (e.g., "Discovery Call", "Online Workshops")
  - Duration/timing
  - What happens during it
  - Who participates
- Use numbered sections for sequential steps
- Be specific about format (online/offline, group size, frequency)

#### Optional Elements to Mention:
- **Report Sample Catalogue**: Pre-built report examples customers can explore
- **Ready-To-Use Report Templates**: Templates that can be customized instantly for their business
- These demonstrate concrete, immediate value

#### Benefits Section
**Purpose**: Clearly state what customers gain

**Guidelines**:
- Focus on outcomes, not just activities
- Use action-oriented language
- Include both tangible deliverables and intangible benefits
- Tangible: "A fully prepared Docentric template", "Documentation of the process"
- Intangible: "A confident team", "Faster onboarding", "Ready to use Docentric effectively"

**Format options**:
- Bullet list with checkmarks (âœ“)
- "Your Outcome" or "What You Get" section
- Numbered list of key benefits

### 4. Call to Action (CTA)
**Purpose**: Make it easy for customers to take the next step

**Guidelines**:
- Start with an action-oriented prompt: "Ready to get started?", "Ready to lift off?"
- Provide multiple contact options:
  - Application/registration link
  - Email address
  - Phone number
  - Calendar booking link
- Make the primary action clear and prominent
- Include the Sales Team contact details

  - Be specific about results achieved

## Partner-Facing Flyer Structure

### 1. Title
Same as customer-facing flyer

### 2. Introduction
**Purpose**: Explain value from partner's perspective

**Key differences from customer version**:
- Focus on partner benefits (revenue opportunity, customer satisfaction, differentiation)
- Explain how workshops help partners serve customers better
- Position as enablement tool for partners
- Address partner pain points (customer onboarding, knowledge transfer, project success)

**Key questions to answer**:
- Why should a partner offer this to their customers?
- What value does it bring to the partner's business?
- How does it make the partner's job easier?

### 3. Body

#### Content Description
- Describe the workshop content (similar to customer version)
- **Add examples**: "Bring them to life using example(s)"
  - Specific customer scenarios
  - Common use cases
  - Success stories

#### Benefits Section
**Important**: List benefits for BOTH partners AND customers

**Partner benefits might include**:
- Faster customer onboarding
- Reduced support burden
- Higher customer satisfaction
- Revenue opportunity
- Differentiation from competitors
- Docentric expertise/certification

**Customer benefits**: Include what customers get (similar to customer-facing version)

### 4. Call to Action (CTA)
**Purpose**: Give partners resources to use with their customers

**Guidelines**:
- Provide TWO links:
  1. Link to the Customer-Facing Workshops Flyer (so partners can share with customers)
  2. Link to Sales Team (for partner support and questions)

## Visual Formatting Best Practices

### Headers
- Use `#` for main title
- Use `##` for major sections
- Use `###` for subsections
- Use `####` for details within subsections

### Lists
- Use `-` or bullet points for unordered lists
- Use numbers for sequential steps
- Use `âœ“` or checkmarks for benefits/outcomes

### Emphasis
- **Bold** for key points, prices, important terms
- *Italics* sparingly, mainly for subtle emphasis
- Use dividers `---` to separate major sections

### White Space
- Leave blank lines between sections
- Don't crowd content
- Keep paragraphs short (2-4 lines)

### Icons/Emojis (use sparingly)
- ðŸ“§ for email
- ðŸ“ž for phone
- ðŸ“… for calendar
- âœ“ for checkmarks/completion
- Only use when they enhance clarity

## Content Tone Guidelines

### Do:
- Use active voice: "You will gain" not "will be gained"
- Address reader directly: "your team", "you finish with"
- Be confident: "You will" not "You might"
- Show results: "A confident team, ready to use Docentric effectively"
- Be specific: "4 sessions Ã— 3 hours" not "several sessions"

### Don't:
- Use jargon without explanation
- Make vague promises
- Write long paragraphs
- Bury important information
- Use passive voice
- Be overly formal or stiff

## Workshop Content Elements to Include

When describing workshop content, consider including:

### Discovery Phase
- Understanding customer needs
- Selecting reports to transform
- Planning the workshop path

### Learning Phase
- Docentric Designer essentials
- Configuration in Dynamics 365 Finance
- Programming and extension patterns
- Hands-on practice

### Support Phase
- Between-session support
- Office hours
- Q&A availability

### Completion Phase
- Final review
- Deliverables handoff
- Next steps
- Certification/recognition

### Deliverables
- Completed templates
- Documentation
- Process guides
- Extra materials agreed during discovery

## Notes for Claude

When helping users create Docentric flyers:

1. **Ask clarifying questions** about:
   - Target audience (customer or partner)
   - Specific workshop content and structure
   - Pricing information
   - Available testimonials
   - Any special features or benefits

2. **Start with structure**: Help outline the flyer before writing content

3. **Iterate section by section**: Don't try to write everything at once

4. **Apply the voice consistently**: Keep checking against the three principles

5. **Suggest improvements**: If you see opportunities to make content more specific, direct, or visually clear

6. **Reference these guidelines**: Point users to specific sections of these instructions when making suggestions
